Jacobian Transforms For Change of 3 Variables

What is a Jacobian transform? In multivariate calculus, we often define cartesian coordinates in terms of polar coordinates, clyndrical coordinates, spherical, etc. Similarly given a region defined by a \( uvw\) we can use the jacobian transformation to redefine it into \(xyz\) space and vice versa.

The proof for the Jacobian transform is for a problem for future self... Soon to come. None the less this is what the general form for change of 3 variables.

Given the following equations \(x=f(u,v,w)\), \(y=g(u,v,w)\), \(z=h(u,v,w)\) the jacobian is the following. \[ \frac{\partial{(X,Y,Z)}}{\partial{(U,V,W)}} = det\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial x}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial x}{\partial v} & \frac{\partial x}{\partial w} \\[1ex] \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial v} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial w} \\[1ex] \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial v} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial w} \end{bmatrix} \]

\[ = \frac{\partial x}{\partial u} det\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial y}{\partial v} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial w}\\[1ex] \frac{\partial z}{\partial v} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial w} \end{bmatrix} - \frac{\partial x}{\partial v} det\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial w}\\[1ex] \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial w} \end{bmatrix} + \frac{\partial x}{\partial w} det\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial v}\\[1ex] \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial v} \end{bmatrix} \]

Problem: Find the Jacobian of the transformation.


\[ x=uw^2\] \[ y = v^3 âˆ’ 3w\] \[ z=\frac{2uv}{w}\]

We first find the partial derrivatives for each of the 3 variables in respect to the the desired transformed variables.

\(\frac{\partial x}{\partial u}= w^2\) \(\frac{\partial x}{\partial v}= 0\) \(\frac{\partial x}{\partial w}= 2uw\)

\(\frac{\partial y}{\partial u}= 0\) \(\frac{\partial y}{\partial v}= 3v^2\) \(\frac{\partial y}{\partial w}= -3\)

\(\frac{\partial z}{\partial u}= \frac{2v}{w}\) \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial v}=\frac{2u}{w}\) \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial w}= \frac{-2uv}{w^2}\)

We can then assemble the matrix and compute the 3x3 matrix determinate for the Jacobian transform.

\[ \frac{\partial{(X,Y,Z)}}{\partial{(U,V,W)}} =w^2 det\begin{bmatrix} 3v^2 & -3\\[1ex] \frac{2u}{w} & \frac{-2uv}{w^2} \end{bmatrix} - 0 det\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial w}\\[1ex] \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial z}{\partial w} \end{bmatrix} + 2uw det\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 3v^2\\[1ex] \frac{\partial z}{\partial u} & \frac{2v}{w} \end{bmatrix} \]

I have purposely left the middle matrix blank as it will zero-out.

\[ = w^2[3v^2(-\frac{2uv}{w^2})+3(\frac{2u}{w})]+2uw[0(\frac{2u}{w})-3v^2(\frac{2v}{w})] \]

I will save the simplification and everything reduces to the following:

\[ \frac{\partial{(X,Y,Z)}}{\partial{(U,V,W)}}= 6u(w-3v^3)\]

Therefore this the Jacobian of the transformation.
