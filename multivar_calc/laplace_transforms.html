Laplace Transforms

Intro: The laplace transform is a method of transforming a function defined in terms of x into a function defined in terms of S. We do these transformations because it is easier to solve certain differential equations in this form. Here is the definition of a transformation of the transformation.

\[\mathcal{L} \{f(t)\} = \int_{0^-}^{\infty} e^{-st}f(t)dt\]

Problem: Transform the following function into its laplacian equivelent.

\[f(t) = \sin(3t)\] \[\mathcal{L} \{f(t)\} = \int_{0^-}^{\infty} e^{-st}f(t)dt\] \[F(t)= \int_{0^-}^{\infty} e^{-st}\sin(3t)dt\]

We preform integrals by parts.\(u=\sin(3t)\) and \(du=3\cos(3t)dt\) also \(dv=e^{-st}\) and thus \(v=\frac{-1}{s}e^{-st}\)

\[=\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) - \int_{0^-}^{\infty} 3\cdot\frac{-1}{s}\cos(3t)e^{-st} dt\] \[=\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) + \frac{3}{s}\int_{0^-}^{\infty} e^{-st}\cos(3t) dt\]

We preform integrals by parts again.\(u=\cos(3t)\) and \(du=-3\sin(3t)dt\) also \(dv=e^{-st}\) and thus \(v=\frac{-1}{s}e^{-st}\)

\[=\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) - \frac{3}{s}[\frac{-1}{s}e^{-st}\cos(3t)-\int_{0^-}^{\infty}-3\sin(3t)\frac{-1}{s}e^{-st}dt ]\] \[=\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) - \frac{3}{s}[\frac{-1}{s}e^{-st}\cos(3t)+\frac{3}{s}\int_{0^-}^{\infty}\sin(3t)e^{-st}dt ]\] \[=\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) + \frac{3}{s^2}e^{-st}\cos(3t)+\frac{9}{s^2}\int_{0^-}^{\infty}\sin(3t)e^{-st}dt ]\]

Notice we can subsitute the values F for the integral at the end.

\[F=\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) + \frac{3}{s^2}e^{-st}\cos(3t)+\frac{9}{s^2}F\] \[(1-\frac{9}{s^2})F=\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) + \frac{3}{s^2}e^{-st}\cos(3t)\] \[F=\frac{\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) + \frac{3}{s^2}e^{-st}\cos(3t)}{(1-\frac{9}{s^2})}\] \[F=\frac{\frac{-1}{s}\cdot \sin(3t) + \frac{3}{s^2}e^{-st}\cos(3t)\Big|_0^{\infty}s^2}{s^2-9}\]

Evaluating the values inside as it approaches 0, we realize that everything zeros out in the cosine term. For the values approaching \(\infty\) we realize that the values all approach zero.

\[F=\frac{ [\frac{3}{s^2}e^{-s(0)}\cos(0)]s^2}{s^2-9}\] \[F=\frac{3}{s^2-9}\]
