Curl

Intro: Curl is often used to describe how much a given point is "curling" in 3 space. Given a vector field how much does that vector field curl? Imagine having a stick flowing in water, the curl describes the tendency to make the stick spin. Curl can be thought of as the circular density of a given point. If the value given by the curl is positive then there is a positive curl. If it is negative that point has a negative curl. If the point has 0 curl then the vector field at that moment is flowing straight. You find the curl by doing the partial derivative via cross product!

\[\vec{F} = \langle P, Q, R \rangle\] \[Curl \vec{F} = \nabla \times \vec{F} \] \[Curl \vec{F} = det\begin{bmatrix} i & j & k \\[1ex] \frac{\partial }{\partial x} & \frac{\partial }{\partial y} & \frac{\partial }{\partial z} \\[1ex] P & Q & R \end{bmatrix} \] \[\nabla \times \vec{F} = (\frac{\partial R }{\partial y}- \frac{\partial Q }{\partial z})i + (\frac{\partial P }{\partial z}- \frac{\partial R }{\partial x})j + (\frac{\partial Q }{\partial x}- \frac{\partial P }{\partial y})k\]

Probelm: Find the curl of the following vector field.

\[\vec{F(x,y,z)} = \langle e^x\sin (y), e^y\sin (z), e^z\sin (x) \rangle\] \[Curl \vec{F} = \nabla \times \vec{F} \] \[Curl \vec{F} = det\begin{bmatrix} i & j & k \\[1ex] \frac{\partial }{\partial x} & \frac{\partial }{\partial y} & \frac{\partial }{\partial z} \\[1ex] e^x\sin (y) & e^y\sin (z) & e^z\sin (x) \end{bmatrix}\] \[Curl \vec{F} = (0-e^y\cos(z))i - (e^z \cos(x)-0)j + (0-e^x\cos(y))k\] \[Curl \vec{F} = -(e^y\cos(z))i - (e^z \cos(x))j - (e^x\cos(y))k\]
