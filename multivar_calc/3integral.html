Triple Integrals

Intro: Triple integrals is simply the volume under the curve \(f(x,y,z)\) or the anti-derivative for the that multivariate function. Regularly we are given an integral over f such that its bounded by the region E

\[\int \int \int_{E}f(x,y,z) dV \]

Problem: Evaluate the triple integral. Where E is bounded under the plane \(z = 1+x+y\) and \(y=\sqrt{x}\), \(y=0\) and \(x=1\)


\[\int \int \int_{E}6xy dV \]

We should firs visualize this region. Since a number of these bounds are determined in the x-y plane, we can see that the bounds \(y=\sqrt{x}\), \(y=0\) and \(x=1\) forms a region as such \(x=1 \) intersects \(yx=\sqrt{x} \) at (1,1). Then we can see that the plane \(z = 1+x+y\) is above the xy plane and thus we can generate these bounds

\[x \in [0,1]\] \[y \in [0,\sqrt{x}]\] \[z \in [0,1+x+y]\]

We can then rewrite the integral as an iterated integral.

\[=\int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{x}} \int_{0}^{1+x+y} 6xy dzdydx \] \[=\int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{x}} [6xyz]\Big|_{0}^{1+x+y} dydx \] \[=\int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{x}} [6xy+6x^2y+6xy^2] dydx \] \[=\int_{0}^{1} [3xy^2+3x^2y^2+2xy^3]\Big|_{0}^{\sqrt{x}} dydx \] \[=\int_{0}^{1} [3x^2+3x^3+2x\sqrt{x}^3] dx \] \[=\int_{0}^{1} 3x^2+3x^3+2x^{\frac{5}{2}} dx \] \[=[x^3+\frac{3}{4}x^4+2\frac{2}{7}x^{\frac{7}{2}}]\Big|_{0}^{1} \] \[= \frac{65}{28}\]

We can also represent each integral in a number of different ways. We should be comfortable with switching between different bounds in respect to different order of integration. The key here is that the inner integrand should contain bounds with the most amount of variables. The outer intergrand should contain none if one wishes to evaluate the definite integral.

Problem: Write the following integral bounded by the following.

\[\int \int \int_{E}f(x,y,z) dV \] \[x+y^2+z^2=1\] \[x=0\]

We know we can rewrite x in terms of the following \(x=1-y^2-z^2\) and since we know x=0 we can write the first bound with that information.

\[x \in [0,1-y^2-z^2]\]

Since we know that x can be 0, we can solve for one of the y, z variables.

\[(0)+y^2+z^2=1\] \[y=\pm\sqrt{1-z^2}\]

This will be our upper and lower bounds for y.

\[y \in [-\sqrt{1-z^2},\sqrt{1-z^2}]\]

Since all we are left with for z is when x and y are 0.

\[(0)+(0)+z^2=1\] \[z=\pm 1\] \[x \in [-1,1]\]

With these bounds we can rewrite the integral in the following.

\[\int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-\sqrt{1-z^2}}^{\sqrt{1-z^2}} \int_{0}^{1-y^2-z^2}f(x,y,z) dxdydz \]
