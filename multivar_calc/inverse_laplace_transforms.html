Laplace Transforms - Differential Equations

Intro: Sometimes we are given a laplace transform and we need to undo the laplace transform. Hahahaha it is difficult... unless you have a table of laplace transforms!! Here is a mini demonstration on how to massage the equation into a transform form.

\[\mathcal{L} \{f(t)\} = \int_{0^-}^{\infty} e^{-st}f(t)dt = F(s)\]

Problem: Find the laplace inverse of the following function.

\[\mathcal{L} \{f(t)\} = \frac{s+3}{s^2+4s+13}\]

Remember on the table we have transforms that are very similar:

\[e^{at}\cos(bt) = \frac{s-a}{(s-a)^2+b^2}\] \[e^{at}\sin(bt) = \frac{b}{(s-a)^2+b^2}\] \[\mathcal{L} \{f(t)\} = \frac{s+3}{s^2+4s+13}\] \[= \frac{(s+2)+1}{(s+2)^2+9}\] \[= \frac{(s-(-2))}{(s-(-2))^2+3^2} + \frac{1}{(s+2)^2+3^2}\] \[= e^{-2t}\cos(3t) + \frac{1}{(s+2)^2+3^2}\frac{3}{3}\] \[= e^{-2t}\cos(3t) + \frac{3}{(s-(-2))^2+3^2}\frac{1}{3}\] \[f(t)= e^{-2t}\cos(3t) + \frac{1}{3}e^{-2t}\sin(3t)\]
