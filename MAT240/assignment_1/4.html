Problem 4

By Peter Ma | Sept 10th 2020

Let \(f:X→X\) be a map of sets. A fixed point of \(f\) is an element \(x∈X\) such that \(f(x)=x\).

a) For each bijection from \(\{1,2,3\}\) to itself, determine the fixed points.

Solution

The fixed points are when \(1 \mapsto 1\) and \(2 \mapsto 2\) and \(3 \mapsto 3\) occur.

A fixed points would be \( (1,1) \) from the first and second map of bijections. \( (2,2) \) from the first and sixth map of bijections. \( (3,3) \) from the first and third map of bijections

\[\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c} \begin{array}{ccc} 1 & \mapsto & 1 \\ 2 & \mapsto & 2 \\ 3 & \mapsto & 3 \end{array} & \begin{array}{ccc} 1 & \mapsto & 1 \\ 2 & \mapsto & 3 \\ 3 & \mapsto & 2 \end{array} &\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & \mapsto & 2 \\ 2 & \mapsto & 1 \\ 3 & \mapsto & 3 \end{array} &\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & \mapsto & 2 \\ 2 & \mapsto & 3 \\ 3 & \mapsto & 1 \end{array} &\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & \mapsto & 3 \\ 2 & \mapsto & 1 \\ 3 & \mapsto & 2 \end{array} &\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & \mapsto & 3 \\ 2 & \mapsto & 2 \\ 3 & \mapsto & 1 \end{array} \end{array}\]
