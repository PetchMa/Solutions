Problem 2

By Peter Ma | Sept 10th 2020

Let \(f:S→T , g:T→U\) and \( h:U→V\) be maps. Prove that composition is associative. \[h \circ (g \circ f) = (h \circ g) \circ f\]

Solution

First we can check the left side of the equation. Let\(s \in S, t \in T, u \in U, v \in V\)

\[h \circ (g \circ f(s))=h \circ (g (f(s))) = h (g (f(s)))\]

Next we can check the right side of the equation. Using the knowledge that \(f:S→T\) and \(t \in T\), \(s \in S\), \(s \mapsto t\) by \(f\) implying that \(f(s) = t\)

\[(h \circ g) \circ f(s))= h(g(t)) \circ f(s) \]

Since \(s \mapsto t\) by \(f\). We can rewrite the following into...

\[ h(g(t)) \circ f(s)= h(g(f(s))) \]

Since left side is equal to right side, we've proven that composition of functions is associative.
