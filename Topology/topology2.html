Induced topology is defined to be the set \(\tau_Y:= \{U\cap Y|U\in \tau\}\) is called the induced topology on \(Y\).

The topological space \((Y, \tau_Y)\) is called a subsapce of \((X,\tau)\ and the elements of \(\tau_Y\) are said to be relatively open subsets of \(Y\) or open in the subspace \(Y\).

Example: consider \(Y = [0,2]\subset \mathbb{R}\) and consider the usual euclidean topology \(\tau = \tau_{eucl}\) on \(\mathbb{R}\) that is \(\tau = T(\mathbb{R}, d_2)\). Then \([0,1) = (-1,1)\cap [0,2]\in \tau_Y\) that is \([0,1)\) is relatively open subset of \(Y\), although it is not open in \(\mathbb{R}\)!

Convergence: A sequence \(\{x_n\}\) of points in \((X,\tau)\) converges to a point \(a\in X\) if \[\forall U(a) \exists N = N(a) \in \mathbb{N}: n>N \rightarrow x_n U(a)\] in this case, \(a\) is a limit of the sequence.

A set \(B(a)\) of open neighborhoods of a is called a local basis at \(a\) if for any open neighborhood \(U(a)\) there exists an element \(B(a) \in B\) such that \(a\in B(a) \subseteq U(a).\)

There are three properties:

    First countable, if it has a countable local basis at each point.
    Second countable if there is a countable basis
    Separable if ti contains a countable dense set.

Theorem: A second countable space is always first countable and separable.

Theorem: A separable metric space is always second countable.

Definition: Connectedness

A subspace Y of a topological space \((X,\tau)\) is said to be connected if the only non-empty clopen sets in \(\tau_Y\) are \(\emtpyset\) and \(Y\). Otherwise its said to be disconnected.

Definition: Path-Connected

A subspace \(Y\) of a topological space \((X,\tau)\) is said to be path connected if any two points \(y_0\) and \(y_1\) in \(Y\) can be connected by a path from \(y_0\to y_1\) in \(Y\)

Path connected implies connected.
