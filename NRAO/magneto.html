Non Thermal Emissions Magnetobremsstrahlung

Larmor's formula states that the acceleration of a charged creates electromagnetic radiation. The magnetic field can also accelerate the charged particle which is known as Magnetobremsstrahlung or magnetic breaking radiation.

For acceleration that is much slower than the speed of light we get gyro radiation. We can use Larmor's formula once again, however with the magnetic field. We know from Ampere's law that the force of magnetic field is the velocity of the particle crossed with the magnetic field lines.

\[\vec{F} = {e(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})\over c}\] \[|\vec{F}| = {e|\vec{v} \times \vec{B}|\over c}\]

We have the F=ma and we know the following...

\[F_c = {mv^2\over r}\] \[a = {v^2\over r}\] \[\omega = {v\over r}\] \[\omega r = {v}\] \[a = {\omega ^2 r}\] \[|\vec{F}| = m|a| = m\omega ^2 r\]

We can sub it into the equation

\[{m\omega ^2 r} = {e|\vec{v} \times \vec{B}|\over c}\] \[{m {v \over r} \omega r} = {e|\vec{v}| |\vec{B}| \sin\theta\over c}\] \[m {v \omega } = {e|\vec{v}| |\vec{B}| \sin\theta\over c}\] \[ \omega = {e |\vec{B}| \sin\theta\over mc}\]

Note all magnetic forces are perpendicular to the velocity vector because it is rotating around circularly thus we can write the following:

\[\omega = { e B \over m c}\]

This is the gyro frequency.
