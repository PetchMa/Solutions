Problem 1.9

For each of the following complex numbers \(z\), find both square roots. Express your answers in cartesian form \(a + bi\).

a) \(z = 2i\)

Convert to polar coordinates.

\[z = 0 + 2i\] \[z = 2[\cos\frac{\pi}{2}+\sin\frac{\pi}{2}i]\] \[z = 2[e^{i\frac{\pi}{2}}]\]

Getting the square root...

\[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2[e^{i\frac{\pi}{2}}}\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2}[e^{i\frac{\pi}{4}}] \] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2}[\cos\frac{\pi}{4} + i\sin\frac{\pi}{4}]\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2}[\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + i\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} ]\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2}\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} [1+ i ]\] \[\sqrt{z }= [1+ i ]\]

Since we have 2 roots, a positive and a negative.

\[\pm\sqrt{z }= \pm[1+ i ]\]

b) \(z = -3i\)

Convert to polar coordinates.

\[z = 0 -3i\] \[z = 3[\cos\frac{-\pi}{2}+\sin\frac{-\pi}{2}i]\] \[z = 3[e^{i\frac{-\pi}{2}}]\]

Getting the square root...

\[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{3[e^{i\frac{-\pi}{2}}]}\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{3}[e^{i\frac{-\pi}{4}}] \] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{3}[\cos\frac{-\pi}{4} + i\sin\frac{-\pi}{4}]\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{3}[\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - i\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} ]\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}} [1- i ]\]

Since we have 2 roots, a positive and a negative.

\[\pm\sqrt{z }= \pm\sqrt{\frac{3}{2}}[1- i ]\]

b) \( 1 + i \sqrt{3}\)

Convert to polar coordinates.

\[z = 1 + i \sqrt{3}\] \[z = 2[\frac{1}{2} + i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}]\] \[z = 2[\cos\frac{\pi}{3}+\sin\frac{\pi}{3}i]\] \[z = 2[e^{i\frac{\pi}{3}}]\]

Getting the square root...

\[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2[e^{i\frac{\pi}{3}}]}\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2}[e^{i\frac{\pi}{6}}] \] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2}[\cos\frac{\pi}{3} + i\sin\frac{\pi}{3}]\] \[\sqrt{z }= \sqrt{2}[\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} - i\frac{1}{2} ]\] \[\sqrt{z }= [\frac{\sqrt{6}}{2} - i\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} ]\]

Since we have 2 roots, a positive and a negative.

\[\pm\sqrt{z }= \pm[\frac{\sqrt{6}}{2} - i\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} ]\]
