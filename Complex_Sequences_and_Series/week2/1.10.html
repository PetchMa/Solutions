Problem 1.10

Use the quadratic formula to factor the following polynomial into linear factors. Express both roots in cartesian form \(a + bi\). \[p(z) = 2z^2 − (1 + i)z − 2i.\]

Solution

Use the quadratic formula in since its in standard form.

\[p(z) =Az^2+Bz+C\] \[p(z) =0\] \[z = \frac{-B \pm \sqrt{B^2-4AC}}{2A}\] \[z = \frac{ − (1 + i) \pm \sqrt{( − (1 + i))^2-4(2)(− 2i)}}{2(2)}\] \[z = \frac{ − (1 + i) \pm \sqrt{ (1 +2i-1)-4(2)(− 2i)}}{2(2)}\] \[z = \frac{ − (1 + i) \pm \sqrt{18i}}{4}\] \[z = \frac{ − (1 + i) \pm 3\sqrt{2}(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}i)}{4}\] \[z = \frac{ − (1 + i) \pm (3+3i)}{4}\]

First root

\[z = -1 - i\]

Second root

\[z_2 = -\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{2}i\]
