Problem 1.11

Check that \(z = 1\) is a root of the following cubic polynomial \[p(z) = z^3 − iz^2 − z + i\]

Solution

Simple polynomial division.

\(z^2+(-i+1)z-i\)
  ________________
\((z-1)|z^3 − iz^2 − z + i\)
\(z^3-z^2\)
  ________________
\((-i+1)z^2-z\)
\((-i+1)z^2-(-i+1)z\)
  ________________
\(-iz+i\)
\(-iz+i\)
  ________________
\(0\)

Therefore \(z=1\) is a solution factor to this equation.

We then want to continue to factor this quadratic with the formula in standard form.

\[z = \frac{-B \pm \sqrt{B^2-4AC}}{2A}\] \[z = \frac{-(-i+1) \pm \sqrt{(-(-i+1))^2-4(1)(-i)}}{2(1)}\] \[z = \frac{-(-i+1) \pm \sqrt{2i}}{2}\] \[z = \frac{-(-i+1) \pm (1+i)}{2}\]

Two solutions

\[z =-1\] \[z =i\]
